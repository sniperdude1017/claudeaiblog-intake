Privacy Policy

This page describes the information collected through claudeaiblog.com, how that information is used, and the operational systems that process signup and attribution data.

Core data

Name, email, phone, address, callback window

Attribution data

Referrer, landing path, UTM tags, `gclid`, `fbclid`, `msclkid`

Last updated

March 26, 2026

What We Collect

Information submitted through the site

When you submit the Claude update form, the site collects the fields currently present in that form: `name`, `email`, `phone`, `address`, `best_time_start`, and `best_time_end`.

The site also records operational lead metadata used by the app, including a generated lead ID, submission timestamp, follow-up priority, routing lane, repeat-submission status, and webhook delivery status.

Attribution

Traffic and campaign information

The site captures traffic source data from the page URL, browser referrer, and landing session so submissions can be traced back to the visit that produced them.

  • `landing_path` and `submission_path`
  • `referrer_url`
  • `utm_source`, `utm_medium`, `utm_campaign`, `utm_content`, `utm_term`
  • `gclid`, `fbclid`, `msclkid`
  • a generated `tracking_session_id` stored in browser storage

Browser Storage

Local and session storage used by the app

The client code stores attribution and conversion state in browser `localStorage` and `sessionStorage` under app-specific keys so the submitted lead can be associated with the current visit and thank-you page flow.

The app is built to support Google Tag Manager, Google Analytics, and Meta Pixel when those IDs are configured at runtime.

The address field is also built to support Google Maps Places autocomplete when a Google Maps API key is configured for the site.

How We Use It

Operational uses of collected information

  • Send or prepare the Claude updates you requested.
  • Route and prioritize follow-up based on submitted lead details.
  • Measure campaign and landing-page performance.
  • Operate webhooks, notes exports, and internal lead workflows.
  • Maintain security, abuse monitoring, and system reliability.

Sharing

When information may leave this app

The app stores lead records in site-controlled operational files and can forward lead data to a configured webhook endpoint. Data may therefore be processed by hosting, infrastructure, analytics, and automation providers used to run the site.

The site does not represent that it sells submitted lead data. Information may also be disclosed when required by law or to protect the service and its operator.

Retention and Requests

How long information is kept and how to contact us

Submitted information is kept for operational use, attribution review, follow-up handling, records, and security review for as long as reasonably needed for those purposes.

To request deletion or ask a privacy question, use the contact email listed on this page and include enough detail to identify the submission.

Quick Facts

  • No payment form is present on this site.
  • The current lead form is U.S.-wide.
  • Admin lead access is protected behind Basic Auth.
  • Lead data can be exported to notes files and webhook endpoints.
